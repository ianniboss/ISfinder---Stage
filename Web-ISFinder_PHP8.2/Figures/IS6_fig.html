The IS6 family.
A) Terminal inverted repeats. B) Transposition mechanism. A target plasmid is distinguished by an open oval representing the origin of replication. The transposon carried by the donor plasmid is composed of two copies of the IS (heavy double lines terminated by small circles) in direct relative orientation (indicated by the open arrowhead) flanking an interstitial DNA segment (shown as a zigzag). The donor plasmid is distinguished by an open rectangle representing its origin of replication. Transposase (Tpase)-mediated replicon fusion of the two molecules generates a third copy of the IS in the same orientation as the original pair (open arrowhead). Homologous recombination using the recA system between any two copies can in principle occur. This will either regenerate the donor plasmid leaving a single IS copy in the target, delete the transposon or transfer the transposon to the target (as shown) leaving a single copy of the IS in the donor molecule.
